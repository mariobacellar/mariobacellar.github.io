What Matters More, Courage or Habit?

Mario Bacellar
Integration Architect Specialist
April 14, 2018

There’s a story (here in Brazil) that in Ceará's state, there was a time when people were marked for death.

During that time, some people decided to eliminate a certain criminal who was terrorizing the region.

So, they called a 'cangaceiro' (a bandit kind of) to kill this criminal, who had quite a résumé of deadly victims.

But before the cangaceiro' accepted the job, they warned him that the mission would be very difficult since this criminal was well-guarded by his henchmen.

The employers then asked the cangaceiro:

"Are you still willing to do it? Do you have the courage for this?"
"To which the cangaceiro replied: Courage, I don’t know... but I do have habit."

When I read this passage from the book "O velho e o menino" (from Roberto Tranjan), I couldn’t help but laugh.

Not long ago, I was doing a job interview over Skype, and my future employers (who had already read my résumé) were only asking me about things (acronyms) that weren’t in my résumé.

In the interview, I answered "...no..." to almost all the questions. So, I thought I wouldn’t get the job.

That’s when they asked me an abstract question, the kind of open-ended question that doesn’t have a right or wrong answer.

Maybe they wanted to hire me (based on my résumé), but needed to hear something that sounded like a YES.

And that’s when my answer was very similar to that of our 'cangaceiro'.

That was a while ago, and I passed the interview.

I was lucky because the interviewers worked in IT and knew how to assess my "habit" (just like the cangaceiro in the story).

I recalled that story now as I read about our cangaceiro, who was just "too used to it"… LOL.

Technology training, at least from what I see in Brazil, is in question!

Academy (university) training teaches you to think (habit), it doesn’t teach you to be a tool (courage) or a set of acronyms!

From my own experience, I know that the "habit" in this story can also come from accumulated experience, where it doesn’t matter (up to a point) how complex the work (technological challenge) the professional will

have to face.

But if the professional only values their courage (IT acronyms), when they run into one they’ve never heard of or an HR interviewer… that’s it… they’ll feel excluded, worthless!

As far as I’m concerned, programming a computer these days is at least 10x LESS COMPLICATED than it was in the 80's !

And that’s very good for the development of society. We need it to be that way. I’m not complaining, no! Times have changed.

This ease exists for many reasons (which could be several posts on their own...).

But to stay on point with our cangaceiro, I’ll just focus on IT acronyms.

With the rise of various frameworks (see Serverless Architecture Patterns) for developing solutions (this could also be several posts…), any web user can choose whether to develop drag-end-drop (no code) or with a

little bit of code, which is merely configuration within some framework. No prejudice, it’s just a fact.

In the U.S., for example, many business users already create their own solutions to analyze data and assist in decision-making. They no longer wait for IT.

They can even create a fairly efficient IT solution. And we all make our living at the end of the day... (well, if that's all that's at stake... fine!)

But just as anyone can fry an egg, there are chefs who make a 'feast' with just an egg and a little seasoning (and that’s intentional).

The question is whether the client just wants to satisfy their hunger or enjoy a meal. Everything in its time! :)

With this myriad of technological acronyms and the most agile management trends, the search for good IT professionals, in my view, is comparable to the 12 labors of Hercules.

Well, if it’s not that complicated for those who 'breathe IT' (like the people who interviewed me), at least I think it must be for the human resources departments.

They don’t even conduct their own tests anymore. That is, HR professionals, for IT companies, merely tick off whether the candidate has this or that acronym on their CV.

It’s the liquid selection [of candidates] of these new times!

So, what matters more, courage or habit?

I don’t know, buddy... courage can fail when faced with a challenge that scares the 'cangaceiro'… LOL.

But the one with the habit has already fulfilled the promise before the spit dries on the ground, in this Brazilian IT Caatinga (in Ceará state, is like Mojave desert).


P.S.: With the liquidity(*) of things being promoted every day, I believe this is also happening in all areas.

(*) Look up: Liquid Love, Liquid World, Liquid Profile (multi-skilled professionals), blah blah Liquid, Liquid is the buzzword, which came to prove its lack of flexibility… haha.
